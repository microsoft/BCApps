# Persuasive Techniques Analysis

Examine a document for persuasive techniques and assess their effectiveness.

## When to Use

- Reviewing marketing copy, proposals, or pitches
- Analyzing speeches, op-eds, or advocacy documents
- Evaluating sales materials or fundraising appeals
- User asks about "persuasion", "rhetoric", or "influence techniques"

## The Three Appeals

### Ethos (Credibility/Authority)
Building trust and establishing expertise

**Techniques:**
- Expert citations and credentials
- Transparency about methodology
- Acknowledging limitations
- Professional tone and presentation

### Pathos (Emotional Appeal)
Connecting through feelings and values

**Techniques:**
- Stories and narratives
- Vivid, sensory language
- Fear or hope appeals
- Identity and belonging appeals

### Logos (Logical Appeal)
Convincing through reason and evidence

**Techniques:**
- Statistics and data
- Logical arguments and syllogisms
- Cause-effect relationships
- Comparisons and analogies

## Additional Techniques

### Rhetorical Devices
- **Repetition**: Reinforcing key points
- **Rhetorical questions**: Engaging the reader
- **Metaphors/Analogies**: Making abstract concrete
- **Rule of three**: Memorable groupings

### Structural Persuasion
- **Problem-Solution**: Creating need then fulfilling it
- **Before-After**: Showing transformation
- **Social Proof**: Citing others' actions/opinions
- **Scarcity**: Emphasizing limited availability
